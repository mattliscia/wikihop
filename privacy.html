Privacy Policy

Last updated: August 2025

Your Privacy Matters

At WikiHop, we believe in complete transparency about how we handle your data. The good news is: we don't collect any of your personal information.

No Data Collection

WikiHop is designed to be completely private and secure. Here's what we don't collect:

  • Personal information (names, emails, addresses)
  • Game scores or statistics
  • Navigation paths or browsing history
  • IP addresses or device information
  • Cookies or tracking data
  • Any other user data

How WikiHop Works

WikiHop operates entirely in your web browser. This means:

  • All game data stays on your device
  • No information is sent to our servers
  • Your gameplay is completely private
  • We cannot see what you're doing

Third-Party Services

WikiHop uses a few third-party services that may collect standard web analytics:

  • Tailwind CSS CDN: Loads styling (may collect basic usage statistics)
  • Wiki APIs: Fetches wiki content (subject to respective wiki privacy policies)

We have no control over these third-party services and their privacy practices.

Browser Storage

Your browser may store some temporary data locally:

  • Game state during your session
  • Challenge links you generate
  • Basic browser cache for performance

This data is stored locally on your device and is not accessible to us.

Children's Privacy

WikiHop does not knowingly collect any personal information from children under 13. Since we don't collect any personal information from anyone, this is not a concern.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date.

Your Rights

Since we don't collect your data, there's nothing to request, modify, or delete. Your privacy is protected by design.

Contact Us

If you have any questions about this privacy policy or our data practices, please contact us through the appropriate channels.

Privacy Summary

TL;DR: WikiHop doesn't collect, store, or process any of your personal data. Everything happens in your browser, and we can't see what you're doing. Your privacy is protected by design.
